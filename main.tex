\section{Introduction}
In this section, $\mathcal{C}$ will denote a 2-category with cartesian products
whose unit will be noted $1$.

We adapt the definitions of \emph{concurrent monoid} from~\cite{rivas2019monads}
to a 2-categorical context. Whiskering forces all monoids in a 2 category to be
ordered.

\begin{definition}
  A \emph{concurrent monoid} consists of:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item An object $M$ of $\mathcal{C}$;
    \item A morphism $e:1\to M$;
    \item Two morphisms $;$ and $||:M\times M\to M$ such that $(M,;,e)$ and
      $(M,||,e)$ are monoids;
    \item A 2-cell $\iota$ such that:

    \item An object $M$ of $\mathcal{C}$;
    \item Two morphisms $\eta:1\to M$ and $\mu:M\times M\to M$ such that $(M,\mu,\eta)$ is a monoid;
    \item Two morphisms $\epsilon:M\to 1$ and $\Delta :M\to M\times M$ such that
      $(M,\delta,\epsilon)$ is a comonoid;
